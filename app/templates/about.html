About RxNorm

Understand the purpose and data behind this drug terminology system.

What is RxNorm?

RxNorm has two main functions: it provides a unified system for naming both generic and branded drugs, and it acts as a facilitator for interoperability between different drug terminologies and pharmacy information systems. Created by the National Library of Medicine (NLM), RxNorm helps ensure clarity and consistency in drug-related data.

Purpose of RxNorm

Healthcare institutions such as hospitals and pharmacies rely on digital systems to manage drug data. These systems often use varying drug names, which can create barriers to effective communication between them. RxNorm addresses this issue by providing a standardized naming system and unique identifiers for medications. Its primary goal is to facilitate clear and efficient exchange of drug information between different systems.
